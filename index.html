What is a Hackathon?

What is a Hackathon?

Hackathons originated in Silicon Valley and are now regularly staged all over the world as a way of rapidly producing ideas and building prototypes. They follow a basic structure - developers, designers and business people form small teams with the goal of designing and implementing a digital product or service prototype to be demonstrated at the end of the event. The lack of time participants have means methods of working can often be chaotic and require "hacking" solutions together, hence the term Hackathon.

Are you going to be hacking into computers?

Are you going to be hacking into computers?

No, there will not be any computer hacking going on. The point of the event is to make things, not break into things.

So this has nothing to do with Hacky Sack?

So this has nothing to do with Hacky Sack?

No, although people are welcome to bring them and play if it helps with brainstorming or relaxing for a bit.

It sounds fun but I don't know anything about programming, so I suppose I'm out...

It sounds fun but I don't know anything about programming, so I suppose I'm out...

Wait! Programming is part of the event, but is really just a technical means to an end. Before the programming happens there needs to be problems identified to be solved and creative ideas generated to tackle them. We need people with ideas and knowledge of other industries to get involved so that together we can figure out how to use technology to solve problems and make awesome things.

What should I build at Hackagong?

What should I build at Hackagong?

Since you've only got a maximum of 30 hours, we're not expecting fully developed and polished products. Most teams will build a small prototype that solves a particular problem or is just really cool. So things like small web and mobile apps, for example this one from last year: Infinite Small. There are various prize categories to work towards, so be sure to checkout the details. Last year's overall winner displayed business viability and are now continuing to operate as a local startup called Giftling.

Who owns what gets made at the event?

Who owns what gets made at the event?

All the intellectual property of anything made belongs to the participating team.

How do we make sure people don't bring in 90% finished projects and pretend they did it all on the weekend?

How do we make sure people don't bring in 90% finished projects and pretend they did it all on the weekend?

Teams should do all the development on the weekend. You're allowed to research the tools/concepts/ideas beforehand. That said, we know a few teams often have some code written prior to the weekend. It's too difficult to police and we don't want to be doing code reviews. If your product is obviously too polished and full featured to have been done in the weekend, the judges will be able to tell and will judge accordingly. Hackagong (and any hackathon) isn't about producing a full featured product, it's about identifying a particular problem and hacking together a prototype of a solution.

What's the situation with food?

What's the situation with food?

We're going to provide all the major meals, along with a continual supply of fruit, instant noodles and energy drink. If you have special dietary requirements you're welcome to bring things too.

Can I bring alcohol in?

Can I bring alcohol in?

No, we're strictly a non-alcohol event. Save that for the party after.

How do I get there?

How do I get there?

Hackagong will be taking place at the uniCentre in Building 11 at the University of Wollongong which is accessible by car, train, bus, bike or foot.

Do I have to be there all weekend?

Do I have to be there all weekend?

No, the doors are unlocked and people can come and go as they please.

Will it be safe to be there overnight?

Will it be safe to be there overnight?

There will be a security guard overnight to keep everyone safe.

What are the sleeping arrangements?

What are the sleeping arrangements?

Most people attempt to work through the night, but some like their sleep as well. We'll provide safe and secure sleeping areas for everyone.

Do you guys provide computers and software?

Do you guys provide computers and software?

We're providing 3D printers for people wanting to build physical stuff. Outside of this, it's generally a BYO gear event. We're happy to make arrangements for people though, get in touch with your discipline ambassador for more details about borrowing gear for the weekend.

Do we need to provide our own internet access?

Do we need to provide our own internet access?

We provide you with a super fast wireless or wired connection to both the internet and a local network for the weekend.
